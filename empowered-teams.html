Why do you need empowered people?

Empowerment provides the conditions for many human needs to be met including autonomy of thought and action, playfulness and creativity, learning and growth, participation and self-expression. Empowerment comes from building partnerships with one another to add value as a system in a way not possible as individuals.

How do we empower our people?

Many strategies are available…

An empowered organisational system is obvious to us when we see and hear it…

  • Top leaders are aware of their own limitations in time and availability. They share their decision making power on key topics to those who know the best.

  • Managers know how to connect the right people together to enable action to take place without getting in the way of those decisions.

  • Team members are curious and ask questions when they don’t understand something, even if “the boss” is in the room. They seek the advice of others because they genuinely care for the input and are then able to make decisions based on that advice.

Despite our appreciation of the value of empowerment, we tend to find ourselves repeatedly in situations where we are not experiencing partnership and therefore feel disempowered. There are typically two different approaches to the loss of empowerment at the most senior levels of organisations:

  • Engage strategy consultants to “solve the problem” and tell your organisation the answer that the unempowered people did not come up with

  • Hire leadership consultants to teach people about how one group of individuals needs to step up more

…Some strategies are better than others

An individual human being is a complex being. There is a lot going on for anyone of us at any given time. An organisational system is a collection of human beings, therefore this system is significantly more complex than any singular human. Any solution seeking to build partnership and empowerment must acknowledge and support members of the system to:

  • See the complex system its basic parts

  • Understand the patterns of relationships within the system

  • See our role in making the change we want to see occur

  • Take action to respond differently to those patterns

Innate-ly supports its clients to experientially gain the above insight and then works side by side with individuals and teams within the organisation by:

  • Sharing alternative ways of working

  • Supporting to teams to experiment with those new ways of working

  • Coaching to hold their system-level perspective in mind as they enact change

What is the Organisational Workshop?

Based on the pioneering work of experiential trainer and organisations expert, Barry Oshrey, the Organisational Workshop provides us with invaluable insight into how the systems that surround us every day work and how we tend to interact with those systems and its members. Whether your family system, the school system your children attend, the workplace you show up to every day during the workweek or the community group you support on weekends… each is a system. And each of us interacts with each system in various roles every day.

What does the workshop help me do?

  • Predict your system

    While all systems are unique, there are consistent conditions and repeat patters of behaviours that emerge. Learn these predictable conditions to give yourself a different choice.

  • Create clarity in how to empower

    Our daily lives are filled with moments of us shifting between roles based on our relationships and place in the system at that moment. Learn how to see where you are in the system and define your stand on how you will operate from that space to empower yourself and others.

  • Build a system based on partnership

    When people are in partnership, they are jointly committed to the success of whatever endeavour, process or project they are in. The OW helps us to see the nature of interactions that lead us into and out of partnership and learn our role in fostering partnership and jointly succeeding with your fellow system members.
